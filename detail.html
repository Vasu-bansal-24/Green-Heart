Care of Nature: Organic Agriculture

The organic garden, obedient to the laws of nature, fosters healthy soil and biological diversity. With non-genetically modified seeds, compost and mulch, the control of pests with natural remedies, and crop rotation, organic agriculture promotes human and planetary health. Without the use of synthetic pesticides or artificial fertilizers, organic food is of exquisite flavor and supports local ecosystems.

Organic farmers, experts in the art of imitating nature, restore soil fertility and reduce erosion. Organically grown plants, stronger and less susceptible to disease, increase biodiversity, which promotes the benefit of pollinating insects and other animals. Organic agriculture, conserving water and reducing pollution, can be sustained for future generations.

From field to table, organic agriculture connects communities around the world. Local organic produce markets flourish, putting farmers and consumers in symbiosis. By consuming organic, you not only protect your health but also our planet.
